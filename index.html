About

Portrait — Emerson Flores

Who I am. I'm Emerson Flores, working as Tecuani Mishpanti — a documentary photographer based in San Salvador, El Salvador.

What I photograph. Markets, barrios, and the historic center of San Salvador, alongside longer-term work in the Dry Corridor and Nahua-Pipil territory.

Why I photograph it. Because the ordinary, in this country, still carries a sacred dimension — and it deserves to be documented as such, not folklorized.

How I work. Slowly, close to my subjects, and always with their consent for how an image will be used.

"I don't understand photography as a record of facts.
I understand it as a way to explore memory, territory, and the ways communities build meaning.
My work documents the places where the everyday still holds a sacred dimension."
— Tecuani Mishpanti
